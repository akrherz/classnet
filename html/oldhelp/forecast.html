Forecast Help

A forecast acts just like a test. It consists of CHOICE, BLANK, OPTION and MULTIPLE questions. Students complete a forecast assignment and submit it for grading. However, each day, the students can submit a new forecast. In addition, the data for the forecast answers are based on the previous day's weather.

To create a forecast, add an assignment, name it and make it a FORECAST. This process will create a set of test items asking about the pertinent information associated with a forecast. You may delete any questions which are not pertinent to your class. You may also limit the number of reporting sites by editing item 1. Do not delete or move item 1 or the forecast will fail. You may also add other questions if you so desire.

When your editing is complete, be sure to edit the forecast assignment (the name at the top), click on the Publish checkbox and Save. This will make the forecast available to students.

Dynamic Answers

You will note that some of the answers use the notation {WIND_DIR_12}. This means that ClassNet will replace this symbol and the brackets with the correct value for the date and site of the forecast when the forecast is graded. These names currently include:
  • WIND_DIR_0 - wind direction (N,NE,E,SE,S,SW,W,NW) at midnight. Acceptable answers may be within 45 degrees of true wind direction. For example, if the wind is

    • from the north, NW,N and NE would all be acceptable.
  • WIND_SPEED_0 - wind speed in knots at midnight (+- 3 knots)
  • TEMP_0 - temperature in Fahrenheit at midnight (+- 5 degrees)
  • DEWP_0 - dew point temperature in Fahrenheit at midnight (+- 5 degrees)
Similar values are available for 6AM, 12PM and 6PM (i.e. 6,12 or 18 after name). In addition,
  • PRECIP - precipitation in inches for the last 24 hours from 12Z.
  • WAS_PRECIP - 1 if there was precipitation and 2 if not.
Reason symbols include:
  • ADVECTION_6 - 1 if 6AM temperature change will be affected by warm air advection, 2 if affected by cold air advection and 3 if none.
  • ADVECTION_12 - same as ADVECTION_6 but related to noon temperature
  • FRONT_6 - 1 if passage of warm front, 2 for cold front, 3 for occluded front and 4 no front
  • FRONT_12 - same as FRONT_6 but related to noon temperature
  • CLOUDINESS_12 - 1 if cloudiness will hold down temperature at noon and 2 if not.
  • RAD_COOLING - 1 if cloud cover to inhibit radiational cooling will be significant, 2 if not
  • FAVOR_PRECIP - factors favoring precipitation may include:

    • 1- moisture supply, 2-frontal position, 3-unstable atmosphere. Any or all answers may
    be selected (e.g. 1 or 1,2 or 1,3 etc.)
